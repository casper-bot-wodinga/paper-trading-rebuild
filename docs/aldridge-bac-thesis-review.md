# Aldridge BAC Thesis Review — July 10, 2026

## Background

Bank of America (BAC) has been a core Aldridge value holding. The thesis rests on
BAC's strong capital position, diversified revenue streams, and attractive
valuation relative to book value. This review examines whether the thesis is
intact given current macro pressures.

## Current Conditions

### Macro Pressure: Rate Reversal

The macro backdrop has shifted significantly. After three rate cuts in late 2025,
markets entered 2026 expecting further easing. Instead:

- **Fed kept rates at 3.50–3.75%** at the June 17, 2026 meeting (no cut).
- **Bank of America economists now forecast 3 rate hikes** before year-end —
  a sharp reversal from the cut-forecast six months ago.
- **10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.48%**, with the 30-year briefly topping 5%.
- Inflation remains sticky, energy costs elevated by the Iran conflict, and the
  labor market resilient (172K jobs added in May 2026).

This rate environment compresses net interest margins in the near term as deposit
costs reprice faster than asset yields.

### Bank Sector: Stress Test Pass

BAC cleared the 2026 Fed stress test (June 24, 2026) alongside Truist and
Wells Fargo. The Fed projected banks could absorb >$700B in losses under a
severe recession scenario. BAC's CET1 ratio remained well above minimums.

The Fed froze Stress Capital Buffer requirements through 2027, enabling banks
to accelerate capital returns via dividends/buybacks. Importantly, the results
will NOT affect capital requirements — a win for the sector.

## Thesis Assessment

**The Aldridge BAC thesis is intact but deserves close monitoring.**

BAC screens well on Aldridge criteria:
- P/E ≈ 14.8x (within Aldridge's < 20x threshold) ✅
- Dividend yield ≈ 2.4% (above 1% minimum) ✅
- Earnings growth ≈ 25% YoY expected (above 5%) ✅
- Debt-to-equity ≈ 1.1x (below 2.0x max) ✅

The rate-reversal risk is real, but BAC's asset-sensitive balance sheet means
rate hikes (if they come) benefit NIM over a 6–12 month horizon. The stress
test pass removes capital overhang.

**Position**: Maintain position. The value case is intact. Watch Q2 earnings
(July 14) as the next catalyst/decision point.
